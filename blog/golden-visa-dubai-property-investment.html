How to Get a Golden Visa Through Dubai Property Investment

The UAE Golden Visa has become one of the most powerful incentives for property investors worldwide. A 10-year renewable residency visa, no employer sponsorship required, with full rights to live, work, and sponsor your family in the UAE. Here is exactly how to qualify through property investment.

What Is the UAE Golden Visa?

Introduced in 2019 and significantly expanded in 2022, the Golden Visa is a long-term residency permit that gives holders 10 years of renewable residency in the UAE. Unlike standard residency visas tied to employment, the Golden Visa is self-sponsored. You do not need a local employer or business partner.

For property investors, this is a major draw. You invest in real estate that generates returns, and you simultaneously gain access to one of the world's most tax-efficient, globally connected countries as a resident.

The Property Investment Threshold: AED 2 Million

To qualify for a Golden Visa through real estate, you must own property in the UAE with a minimum value of AED 2 million (approximately USD 545,000). This threshold applies to the purchase price of the property as stated on the title deed, not the current market value.

Key Rules for the AED 2M Threshold

  • Multiple properties count. You do not need a single property worth AED 2 million. You can combine multiple properties to reach the threshold. For example, two apartments worth AED 1 million each will qualify.
  • Off-plan properties are eligible. As of the 2022 updates, off-plan properties qualify for the Golden Visa, provided the total value meets the AED 2 million threshold. This was a significant change that opened the door for off-plan investors.
  • Mortgaged properties qualify. You do not need to own the property outright. Even if you have a mortgage, the full purchase price counts toward the threshold, not just the equity you have paid.
  • Freehold areas only. The property must be in a designated freehold area. This includes most popular investment zones like Dubai Marina, Downtown, JVC, Business Bay, Dubai Hills, Palm Jumeirah, and Dubai Creek Harbour.

Benefits of the Golden Visa

The Golden Visa offers far more than just residency. Here is what you gain as a property investor with Golden Visa status:

  • 10-year renewable residency. No need to renew every 2-3 years like standard residence visas. The visa is valid for 10 years and can be renewed indefinitely as long as you maintain ownership.
  • No minimum stay requirement. You can spend as much or as little time in the UAE as you want. There is no 6-month residency rule. This is ideal for investors who live abroad but want UAE residency benefits.
  • Sponsor your family. The visa covers your spouse, children (regardless of age), and domestic helpers. Your family does not need separate qualification.
  • 0% income tax. The UAE has no personal income tax. Rental income from your property is completely tax-free.
  • 0% capital gains tax. When you sell your property at a profit, you keep 100% of the gain.
  • UAE banking access. As a resident, you can open local bank accounts, access mortgages, and integrate into the UAE financial system.
  • Emirates ID. You receive a national identity card, simplifying everything from mobile phone contracts to business setup.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Step 1: Purchase Qualifying Property

Acquire freehold property (or properties) in Dubai with a total value of at least AED 2 million. This can be off-plan or ready. Ensure your Sales and Purchase Agreement (SPA) and title deed documentation are in order.

Step 2: Obtain Your Title Deed

For ready properties, you will receive a title deed from the Dubai Land Department (DLD) at the time of transfer. For off-plan properties, an Oqood (initial registration) certificate is issued. Both documents are accepted for Golden Visa applications.

Step 3: Apply Through ICP or GDRFA

Applications are submitted through the Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs, and Port Security (ICP) portal or the General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs (GDRFA) in Dubai. Many applicants use the ICP smart app for convenience.

Step 4: Submit Required Documents

You will need the following documents:

  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity)
  • Title deed or Oqood certificate
  • Health insurance valid in the UAE
  • Passport-sized photographs
  • Proof of financial solvency (bank statements)
  • Medical fitness test results (completed in the UAE)

Step 5: Medical Fitness and Emirates ID

Once your application is provisionally approved, you will complete a medical fitness test at an approved center in the UAE and have your biometrics taken for the Emirates ID. This typically takes 1-2 business days.

Step 6: Visa Stamping

After all steps are complete, your Golden Visa is stamped in your passport. The entire process from application to visa stamping typically takes 2-4 weeks, though processing times can vary.

Common Questions

Can I Apply From Outside the UAE?

The initial application can be started online, but you will need to enter the UAE for the medical fitness test and biometrics. Many investors combine the application process with a property inspection trip.

What Happens If I Sell the Property?

Your Golden Visa remains valid even if you sell the property, as long as the visa is already issued. However, at renewal time (after 10 years), you will need to still meet the qualification criteria. Many investors plan their portfolio to always maintain the AED 2 million threshold.

Can I Work on a Golden Visa?

Yes. The Golden Visa allows you to live and work in the UAE. You can be employed, self-employed, or start a business. There is no restriction on professional activity.

Strategic Considerations for Investors

The Golden Visa creates an interesting dynamic for property investment strategy. Since the threshold is AED 2 million, many investors target this exact price point to achieve both investment returns and residency benefits. A well-chosen property at AED 2 million in an area like Dubai Hills Estate or Jumeirah Beach Residence can deliver 6-7% net rental yield while simultaneously securing a 10-year visa.

For investors with larger budgets, the Golden Visa becomes a complementary benefit rather than the primary driver. But at the AED 2 million entry point, it effectively makes your property investment double as a lifestyle asset, giving you and your family access to world-class healthcare, education, safety, and connectivity.

With 0% property tax, 0% capital gains tax, and 0% income tax, the total cost of ownership in Dubai is dramatically lower than comparable global cities like London, Singapore, or Hong Kong.
