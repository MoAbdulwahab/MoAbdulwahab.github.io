Key Terms Explained

Purchase PriceThe agreed sale price of the property — not including transaction fees.
DLD Fee (4%)Dubai Land Department transfer tax — paid once on purchase. Standard rate is 4% of the purchase price.
Agent Commission (2%)Broker fee paid to the selling agent on completed (ready) properties. Typically 2% in Dubai.
Down PaymentThe upfront portion of the purchase price paid to the developer on signing. Typically 10–25% for off-plan.
Construction PaymentsInstalments paid to the developer during the build phase, usually tied to construction milestones.
Post-Handover BalanceThe remaining purchase price paid after you receive the keys, spread over a developer payment plan (1–10 years).
Capital AppreciationHow much the property value grows per year, as a percentage. Dubai's 10-year average is 6–12% depending on area.
Gross Rental YieldAnnual rent ÷ purchase price × 100. A quick measure of income return before costs.
Net Rental YieldAnnual rent minus running costs (service charge, management) ÷ purchase price × 100. The real income return.
Service ChargeAnnual fee paid to the building's management for upkeep of common areas. Billed per sq ft — typically AED 10–35/sqft in Dubai.
Cash-on-Cash ReturnNet annual income ÷ total capital invested. Measures how hard your cash is working each year.
ROIReturn on Investment — total profit divided by total capital deployed, expressed as a percentage.
Tenant Coverage RatioHow much of your post-handover instalment is covered by rental income. 100%+ means the tenant fully funds the payment.
Net Cash From PocketThe total you personally paid after rental income offsets post-handover instalments. Your true out-of-pocket cost.
